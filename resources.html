About Resources ☰
  • Asian Pacific Fund: The Asian Pacific Fund aims to restore AAPI communities devastated by harmful effects of the COVID-19 pandemic, including the steep rise in AAPI hate.
  • Color of Change: As the nation's largest online racial justice organization, Color of Change aims to strengthen the political voice of Black communities by tackling injustice in all areas of life.
  • United We Dream: This nonprofit youth-led organization fights to empower young immigrant voices, regardless of their legal status. They provide resources regarding citizenship, DACA, deportation, COVID-19, and more.
  • Centro SOL Summer Scholars Program: This program is designed for high school students fluent in both Spanish and English and works to expose them to the healthcare field. Selected scholars get to work with JHUSOM faculty in clinical settings that serve Latino patients with limited-English proficiency.
  • Asian American Studies Program: This program is designed to help individuals educate and understand the history and culture of Asian Americans. They study and learn from people who have immigrated and have ancestral ties to any region of Asia and the Pacific.
  • African American Health Program: This program provides free, critical health services for African Americans. They provide education on different topics such as Maternal and Infant Health, Cancer, Mental Health, and so much more. The AAHP helps groups of all ages from the infants to the seniors.
  • Muchacha: Muchacha is owned and founded by Diana Hoyos, a Latino Woman. They sell different types of coffee and mugs that show and encourage women empowerment. Diana's goal is to show her representation for Latino's and Women all in one.
  • Alodia: Alodia is owned and founded by Isfahan Chambers-Harris, a Black Woman. They sell different types of oils, shampoos, conditioners, etc. They have a quiz available on their website to create the perfect hair products for you. They have multiple categories for men, women and kids. Isfahan's goal is to educate women on their natural hair.
  • Silk and Sonder: Silk and Sonder is owned and founded by Meha Agrawal, an Asian American woman. They sell month subscriptions of journals with positive promts. Meha's goal is to solve the emotional health epidemic for customers versus being a band-aid fix.
