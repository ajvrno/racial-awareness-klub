EDUCATION INEQUALITY

Inequal access to education among racial groups is not something new to us. As late as the 1960s, we've heard of schools segregated by race, with these schools being funded much lower than white-serving schools. While school segregation has long been outlawed thanks to Brown v. Board, unequal opportunities and access to education still remain. According to the NCES in 2015, around 60-75% of AAPI K-12 students felt that they were going to a school where less than 25% of students were there own ethnicity. Furthermore, predominantly-minority schools often have a larger student body size, curriculum and learning materials are lower in quality, and teachers are less qualified in the fields they teach.
